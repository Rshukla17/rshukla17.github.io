Research

Working papers and research interests

My research spans financial markets, health care analytics, uncertainty modeling and academic integrity in digital learning platforms. I work with econometric models, causal inference frameworks and modern machine learning to answer questions that matter for policy and practice.

I am particularly interested in Uncertainty Quantification, Bayesian Methods, Causal Inference, Graph Theory, Stochastic Simulations and its application to real world.

Research interests
Econometrics and financial markets Causal inference Bayesian networks and neural networks Uncertainty quantification Applied machine learning
Analyzing Financial and Overall Performance of Nursing Homes
First author, supervised by Dr. Raju Mainali · Under review at Journal of Economic Modeling
Uses CMS data and econometric models such as ordinary least squares, fixed effects and two stage least squares to evaluate financial and operational performance of nursing homes and identify determinants of care quality and financial sustainability.
Real World Effectiveness and Safety of Inclisiran in Clinical Practice
Co author, in collaboration with Mercy Hospital · Submitted to American College of Cardiology
Statistical evaluation of inclisiran for LDL cholesterol reduction using patient level data, including efficacy analysis and replication of published real world studies.
Granger Causality and Random Walk Efficiency in Financial Indices
First author · Under review
Studies interdependencies between overall market, banking and insurance indices using Granger causality and random walk tests to evaluate market efficiency and sector dynamics.
Causal Inference in Multi Asset Pricing Models Using Bayesian Networks
First author, supervised by Dr. Raju Mainali · Work in progress
Applies Bayesian networks to uncover causal relationships across equities, bonds and commodities to improve multi class asset pricing and systemic risk assessment.
Automated Cheating Detection in Learning Management Systems
Literature review · Under review
Comprehensive survey of artificial intelligence and machine learning methods for detecting academic dishonesty in learning management systems and proposal of privacy aware proctoring solutions.
Improving Mortgage Risk Assessment with Bayesian Neural Networks
First author · Work in progress
Uses Bayesian neural networks, deep ensembles and causal inference on credit risk datasets to improve probability calibration, uncertainty estimation and decision support for mortgage default prediction.
Predictive Modeling of Social Media Virality Using Stochastic Processes
Course project
Simulated data from X, then applied Hawkes processes, Markov chains, Poisson processes and random forest models to study tweet virality and engagement dynamics, achieving around 68 percent accuracy and identifying key engagement predictors.
